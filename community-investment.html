Construction Compliance Initiative

With the construction of the Comer Children’s Hospital in 2001, UChicago Medicine established the Construction Compliance Initiative. The purpose of the initiative is to encourage economic development with contracting opportunities for minority and woman-owned business enterprises.

From 2001 to 2023, certified minority and woman-owned firms (M/WBE) achieved $531.4 Million in economic benefit from contracts awarded and paid on UChicago Medicine capital, renovation and plant expenditures.

Fiscal Year 2023
  • $32.6 million in contracts awarded and paid to certified minority-and woman-owned firms.
  • $1.3 million in wages paid to minority and female onsite construction workers
  • $730,000 in wages earned by 192 Chicago residents working on UChicago Medicine construction projects
  • $531.4 million in economic benefits to certified minority and woman-owned firms on UChicago Medicine capital and renovation projects (2001 -2023)

Local Firms: In fiscal year 2023, 23 local firms worked on UChicago Medicine construction projects.

Supplier Diversity
  • In fiscal year 2023, more than $28 million in contracts were awarded and paid to minority- and woman-owned non-construction-related vendors and service providers.
  • More than $150 million were awarded and paid to firms with offices in the State of Illinois.
